Ana Roqué de Duprey

Ana Roqué de Duprey, also known as "Flor del Valle", was born 1853 in Aguadilla, Puerto Rico. She was an educator, writer, scientist, and ffounded the first woman’s suffrage organization in Puerto Rico in 1917. Roqué excelled in school from a young age and graduated from the age of nine. From there she studied botany, zoology, astronomy, geology, and meteorology. At age 13 she opened her own schools and taugh from textbooks she wrote. In 1893, Roqué founded the newspaper La Mujer (“The Woman”), the first publication on the island designed specifically for women.

Some of her achievements include:

  1. In 1932, the University of Puerto Rico awarded Roque an honorary doctorate degree. She was also named honorary president of the Puerto Rican Liberal Party.
  2. In 1933, Ana Roqué Géigel de Duprey died in Río Piedras, and a memorial has been done to honor her life. A high school in Humacao, Puerto Rico and a street in San Juan are named for her. An elementary school in Chicago, Illinois, which has a large Puerto Rican population, is named after her.
  3. Roque was posthumously named an honoree of the National Women's History Alliance in 2020.
